About

Training across data science, public health, and infectious disease

I am an infectious disease epidemiologist with interdisciplinary training in data science, epidemiology, and systems modeling. My work spans epidemic modeling and phylodynamics, suicide epidemiology, opioid use disorder, and applications of natural language processing to social determinants of health.

I am currently a PhD student in Epidemiology (Infectious Disease track) at the University of California, Berkeley, and previously completed my MS in Epidemiology at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. I am particularly interested in how quantitative methods and mechanistic models can inform real-world decision-making in infectious disease control and mental health.
